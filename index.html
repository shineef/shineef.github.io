About Me

I am currently pursuing a Master of Science in Computer Science at Worcester Polytechnic Institute. My academic and research interests are primarily focused on Machine Learning, Artificial Intelligence, and Cloud Computing, among other emerging technologies in the field of computer science.

Projects

Reinforcement Learning

I have developed and implemented a range of algorithms designed for autonomous mastery in gaming applications. These include the Markov Decision Process, Monte Carlo methods, Temporal Difference learning, and Deep Q-Networks (D3QN), among others. This work focused on leveraging these sophisticated techniques to enhance decision-making and strategy formulation in complex game environments. A significant aspect of my work involved enhancing the replay memory mechanism and other components of the DQN method, thereby improving the overall efficacy and performance of these machine learning models in complex simulations.

Information Retrieval

In addition to my work on autonomous gaming algorithms, I have also developed recommender systems utilizing both item-based and user-based collaborative filtering techniques. These systems were designed using various similarity metrics to optimize recommendations. Furthermore, I have integrated deep learning methods to enhance the accuracy and efficiency of these systems, showcasing a blend of traditional filtering approaches with advanced neural network models.

Computer Vision

With a background in image processing and pattern recognition, I have mastered advanced techniques such as Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) and Transformers. My competence in image processing includes the ability to discern symbols within images and adeptly utilize classification algorithms like KNN and Rocchio. Additionally, my portfolio includes diverse projects, such as handwriting recognition, where I have applied these skills to achieve precise and effective outcomes.
